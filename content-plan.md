# Content & Traffic Plan — The Ledger (NJ Online Casino Guide)

Adapt "New Jersey" to whichever state/market you actually pick — the structure holds either way.

## Why this niche over "best online casino"
Broad head terms are owned by sites with years of authority and huge link budgets. State-specific,
compliance-literate content has real search volume, much less competition, and is the exact kind of
page Google trusts for YMYL (Your Money or Your Life) topics like gambling — because it demonstrates
expertise on the actual legal framework, not just marketing copy.

## Three content pillars

### 1. Operator reviews (bottom-of-funnel, converts)
One page per licensed operator, using `review-template.html`. These are the pages that carry your
affiliate links. Keep them honest and specific — DGE/regulator license number, real tested payout
time, real wagering terms in plain English. This is also what separates a page that ranks from one
Google treats as thin affiliate spam.

### 2. Comparison & "best of" pages (mid-funnel)
- Best online casinos for slots in [state]
- Best live dealer casinos in [state]
- Fastest payout online casinos in [state]
- [Operator A] vs [Operator B]: which is better in 2026
- Best casino welcome bonuses this month (update monthly — freshness matters here)

### 3. Educational guides (top-of-funnel, builds authority + backlinks)
- How wagering requirements really work *(built — see guide-welcome-bonuses.html)*
- Is online gambling legal in [state]? Full legal breakdown
- How we verify a casino's license (your trust/methodology page — link this from every review)
- Online casino vs. sweepstakes casino: what's the legal difference
- How casino payout times actually work (ACH vs. e-wallet vs. check)
- Responsible gambling tools explained: deposit limits, self-exclusion, time-outs
- Taxes on gambling winnings in [state]: what you actually owe
- Are online casino apps different from the desktop site?

## SEO basics specific to this niche
- **E-E-A-T matters more here than almost any other niche.** Google explicitly treats gambling as
  YMYL content. A visible author, a methodology page, and evidence you actually verify license data
  will outperform generic affiliate copy.
- **Internal linking**: every review should link to the methodology page and the relevant comparison
  hub; every guide should link to 2-3 relevant reviews.
- **Freshness**: bonus terms and license status change. Add a visible "last verified" date to every
  review and actually update it monthly — stale bonus data is a fast way to lose trust and rankings.
- **Schema markup**: use Review and FAQ structured data once you have real content — it improves how
  listings show up in search.
- **Backlinks**: gambling is a hard link-building niche because of Google's own guidelines on paid
  links. Focus on digital PR (original data — e.g., "we tracked payout times across 11 NJ operators
  for 60 days"), guest contributions to legal/finance sites, and organic citations from your
  methodology transparency.

## Before you publish anything
- Replace every bracketed placeholder in `review-template.html` with data you have personally
  verified against the operator's current terms and your state's regulator registry.
- Confirm your own registration/licensing obligations as an affiliate in your target state —
  some regulators (NJ DGE included) have rules for how affiliates may advertise licensed operators.
- Add an accurate advertising disclosure and only link to operators actually licensed in the
  state(s) you're targeting.
